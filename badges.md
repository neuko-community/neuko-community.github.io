## Community Distribution Story

### The Social Experiment

The Snake badge distribution was designed as a social experiment to test community values and loyalty:

**Setup**:
- Team airdropped Snake badges to Rabbit and Moth holders
- Gave holders complete freedom: Hold, Sell, or Give
- No instructions on who to give to
- No team oversight or control

**Community Response**:
- Most holders chose to give badges to community members
- Rewarded those who:
  - Interacted and engaged
  - Created content
  - Showed support
  - Helped onboard others
- Some bought badges on secondary markets to gift to others
- Community self-organized distribution channels

**Results**:
- Successful decentralized distribution
- Strong community cohesion
- Low sell pressure (2-3% listed)
- High conviction and loyalty
- Community-driven onboarding

**Significance**: Demonstrated that community values engagement and contribution over quick profits

**Source**: `sources/thread-1.md`, `sources/thread-2.md`, `sources/thread-3.md`

## Badge Philosophy

### Transferable by Design

From founder @SQU1NCH:
- Badges are fully transferable, sellable, "do-whatever-you-wantable"
- Team considered soul-bound tokens but chose transferability
- "Everything has a price" - free market approach
- Holders are free to sell if they want

### Meaning Over Utility

- Badges mean "you were here first"
- Badges mean "you believed early"
- "This is your in" - credential for early supporters
- Value comes from community and conviction, not forced utility

### Community Freedom

- No forced holding
- Free market is the best market
- Community decides value
- "Just be good to each other and stay safe"

**Source**: `sources/founders/squinch-post-1.md`
